# Daniel Wishnia

Most enterprise AI projects don't fail at the demo. They fail three months
later, when the pilot impressed everyone and then nobody opened the tool
again. Closing that gap is the work I do.

Right now I'm leading GenAI adoption inside a publicly listed insurance
group. Copilot 365, custom agents, and the role-based training that decides
whether a rollout sticks or quietly dies. Twelve-plus business units,
measured on real usage rather than licenses bought.

I've been inside this problem for almost 20 years, on the operator side, not
the slideware side. CDTO at Aroundtown SA, MDAX-listed, one of Europe's
largest real estate groups, where I owned the digital and AI agenda from data
architecture through VR staging to GenAI rollouts. Before that, nine years as
Director of Digital and eCommerce at GCH Hotel Group, 120 hotels across seven
countries, including the sector's first VR travel portal. Same thread every
time. I turn "AI sounds important" into something that ships and that people
actually use.

I also tend to see the shift early. In 2005 I was calling it Digital Sense,
the idea that commerce, learning and creativity would stop being things you
click through and become things you talk to. Sounded far-fetched then. It's
the default now.

## What I'm working on

The citation economy. Brand value is drifting away from traffic and toward
how often AI systems name you as the answer when a customer asks. Most
companies have no idea whether ChatGPT, Gemini or Perplexity point to them or
to a competitor. I coined the term, built the audit methodology, and
developed the frameworks organizations use to act on it. GEO and AEO, the
competitive layer most teams aren't tracking yet.

## What's here
